About me

Hello! I'm Stefania, an Italian freelance journalist and writer currently based in the southwest of England - though I'm often in Milan visiting my family. I hold a degree in literature from the University of Milan and a diploma in Freelance and Feature Writing from the London School of Journalism.

After my graduation I worked in Italy as a freelance journalist for a local newspaper. This experience taught me the importance of fieldwork: I listened and talked to people, observing almost like an anthropologist.

Since arriving in England in 2015 I've dived headfirst into British life. I enjoy writing about the differences between Italian and British culture, making fun of Italian stereotypes and my family's vicissitudes (obviously in English, so they don't understand!).

I consider myself a versatile writer who likes dealing with different topics. My aim is to write meaningful articles that can make a difference for my readers. I want my writing to be a form of criticism because I see journalism as a constant attempt at generating awareness of social issues.
